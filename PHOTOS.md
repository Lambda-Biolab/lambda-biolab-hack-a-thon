# Photos

Images under [`public/photos/`](./public/photos) are included in this
repository **for use by this site only** (Lambda Hack Basel 2026 at
lambda-biolab.github.io/lambda-biolab-hack-a-thon/).

They are **not** covered by the [MIT `LICENSE`](./LICENSE) that governs the
source code.

## What you can do

- Clone, build, and deploy this repository as-is for the Lambda Hack Basel
  event.
- Reference these photos in documentation about the event (with attribution
  to Lambda Biolab).

## What you can't do

- Reuse the photos in a different site, template, or product without explicit
  written permission from Lambda Biolab and, where applicable, from the
  identifiable individuals depicted.
- Redistribute the photos as part of a fork or template repository.

## If you fork this repo as a template

Please supply your own imagery. Delete the contents of `public/photos/` and
replace with photos you own or have licensed. The code references photo
paths via arrays in `src/components/Hero.tsx`, `src/components/LabStrip.tsx`,
and `src/components/Tracks.tsx` — update those to match your filenames.

## Why this matters

Many of these photos depict identifiable people (Lambda Biolab members,
collaborators, event participants). Under Swiss data protection law and
general photography rights, an MIT grant on source code does not carry
through to a model-release for their likeness. Keeping photos out of the
code license makes the constraint unambiguous.
